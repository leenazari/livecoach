INTERVIEW QUESTION FRAMEWORK — competency-based screening

This framework guides which competencies to probe and how to deepen weak or
vague answers. Use it to suggest the next best question.

COMPETENCY 1 — OWNERSHIP & IMPACT
Look for evidence the candidate drove outcomes, not just participated.
- "Walk me through a project where you owned the outcome end to end."
- "What would have happened if you hadn't been on that project?"
- Probe if vague: "What specifically did YOU decide, versus the team?"
- Probe for impact: "How did you measure success? What was the number before
  and after?"

COMPETENCY 2 — TECHNICAL DEPTH
Test the floor of their knowledge, then push one level past comfort.
- "Take me through the hardest technical decision you made recently."
- "What was the trade-off, and what did you give up by choosing that?"
- Probe if shallow: "Why that approach over the obvious alternative?"
- Stress test: "Where would that solution break at 10x the scale?"

COMPETENCY 3 — PROBLEM SOLVING UNDER AMBIGUITY
- "Tell me about a time the requirements were unclear. What did you do first?"
- "How did you decide what to build when no one could tell you the answer?"
- Probe: "What did you get wrong in that process, and how did you catch it?"

COMPETENCY 4 — COLLABORATION & CONFLICT
- "Describe a disagreement with a colleague about an important decision."
- "How did it resolve? Looking back, were you right?"
- Probe for self-awareness: "What would the other person say about you?"

COMPETENCY 5 — LEARNING & GROWTH
- "What's something you believed strongly a year ago that you've since changed
  your mind on?"
- "How do you get better at things outside of your day job?"

GENERAL PROBING PRINCIPLES
- If an answer is generic, ask for a specific recent example with names, numbers,
  and dates.
- If an answer is impressive, verify ownership: separate "I" from "we".
- If a competency is already well-evidenced, move on — don't over-mine one area.
- Always prefer questions that build on what the candidate just said.
